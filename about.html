About

Christopher M. Russo is the Republican Chief Economist for the U.S. Congress Joint Economic Committee. His work spans the full range of the Committee’s jurisdiction. He briefs members on economic conditions; advises on economic policy and legislation; and supports oversight of the Treasury Department, Federal Reserve, SEC, and other agencies. He previously served as economist for the U.S. Senate Banking Committee.

Mr. Russo is also a doctoral student in economics at George Mason University, where his research focuses on monetary economics and economic history. He was a postgraduate research fellow with the Mercatus Center, an economics research center at George Mason. Before Mercatus, he advised top policymakers at the New York Fed and Chicago Fed on monetary policy and financial markets.

Russo has been published by prominent outlets including National Review, The Hill, Barron’s, and Reason. His views have been quoted by Dow Jones The Wall Street Journal, CNBC, Reuters, S&P Global, and other leading news organizations. Russo has also regularly appeared in live broadcast media, such as C-SPAN Washington Journal and Bloomberg TV. He has testified before the U.S. House Financial Services Committee about his expert views on the Federal Reserve’s emergency lending programs.

Russo earned a master’s degree in financial mathematics from the University of Chicago. He completed his bachelor’s degree in mathematics and economics at Rutgers University, New Brunswick.
