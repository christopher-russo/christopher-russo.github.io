About

Christopher M. Russo is a doctoral student in economics at George Mason University. His academic research focuses on monetary economics and economic history. On his journey from GED to PhD, he earned an associate's degree from Raritan Valley Community College, a bachelor's degree from Rutgers University, and a master's degree in financial mathematics from the University of Chicago.

Mr. Russo was the Republican chief economist for the U.S. Congress Joint Economic Committee. As the lead economic advisor for Congressional Republicans, he briefed members and staff on economic conditions; advised on economic policy and legislation; and supported oversight of the Treasury Department, Federal Reserve, SEC, and other agencies. He previously advised top policymakers with appointments at the U.S. Senate Banking Committee, Federal Reserve Bank of New York, and Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago.
